Nachos

These homemade nachos are great for game day. This recipe makes a huge meal-sized tray of nachos with lots of good stuff! You can adjust ingredient quantities to suit your preference. Serve with extra chips if required.

Ingredients

  1. Aluminum foil
  2. 1 pound ground beef
  3. 3/4 cup water
  4. 1 (1.25 ounce) package taco seasoning mix
  5. 1 (18 ounce) package restaraunt-style tortilla chips
  6. 1 cup shredded sharp Cheddar cheese, or more to taste
  7. 1 (15.5 ounce) can refried beans
  8. 1 (10 ounce) can pitted black olives, drained and chopped
  9. 1 cup salsa
  10. 1 cup sour cream, or more to taste
  11. 4 green onions, diced
  12. 1 (4 ounce) can sliced jalapeno peppers, drained

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven broiler. Set the oven rack about 6 inches from the heat source. Line a baking sheet with aluminum foil.
  2. Gather all ingredients.
  3. Cook and stir ground beef in a skillet over medium heat until meat is crumbly and no longer pink, 5 to 10 minutes. Drain excess grease. Stir in water and taco seasoning mix; simmer until beef mixture has thickened, 8 to 10 minutes.
  4. Spread tortilla chips on the prepared baking sheet. Top with Cheddar cheese, then dot with refried beans and ground beef mixture.
  5. Broil in the preheated oven until cheese is melted, 3 to 5 minutes. Top nachos with olives, salsa, sour cream, green onions, and jalape√±o peppers.
